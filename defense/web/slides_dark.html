01 / Problem & Motivation

Three reasons this is hard

No closed form

Coupled nonlinear ODEs like Lorenz-1960 rarely admit an analytical solution.

Solvers are costly

RK4 is accurate but mesh-dependent — and must re-solve step-by-step for every new query.

Neural nets lean on physics

Most neural ODE methods add physics-informed losses or operator learning to get there.

Central question: can a plain feedforward ANN — trained only on numerical data, no physics in the loss — approximate the Lorenz-1960 solution, and which architecture does it best?

02 / Objectives

What we set out to do

Build a trusted reference

Solve Lorenz-1960 with RK4 and SciPy, then cross-validate the two solvers.

Search the architecture space

Systematically vary depth, width, and activation — plain ANN, no physics aids.

Measure accuracy vs cost

MSE, MAE, max error, plus training and inference time and convergence stability.

Benchmark vs literature

Position the best plain ANN against PINN / DeepONet results from prior work.

03 / Background

The Lorenz-1960 system

ẋ = −0.10 y z
ẏ = +1.60 x z
ż = −0.75 x y
k = 2, l = 1  ·  x₀=0.5, y₀=0.75, z₀=1  ·  t ∈ [0, 1]

Three coupled nonlinear ODEs — an early meteorological model. Compact, but a genuinely demanding benchmark for any solver.

Long-term evolution over t ∈ [0, 50] — bounded, quasi-periodic orbits.

03 / Background

Plain ANN vs Physics-Informed NN

Plain ANN — data-driven, learns t → (x, y, z) by backpropagation.
PINN — embeds the governing equations directly in the loss.
Our scope: the plain ANN on the left. PINNs appear only as a literature benchmark — we do not implement them.

03 / Gap & Questions

The gap we fill

The gap
  • No architecture study for Lorenz-1960
  • No PINN vs plain-ANN comparison on it
  • No systematic activation-function study
  • Depth and width rarely varied together
Research questions
  • Can a plain ANN learn the solution from numerical data alone?
  • Which depth × width × activation gives the best accuracy–cost trade-off?
  • How close does it come to physics-informed baselines?
