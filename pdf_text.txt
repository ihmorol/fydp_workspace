Solving the Lorenz-1960 ODE System
Using ANN and Identifying the Optimal Architecture
Department of CSE, United International University
FYDP-I research proposal 
Problem statement: Can a plain ANN learn the Lorenz-1960
solution accurately, and which ANN architecture works best
when compared against reliable numerical and PINN-style
references?

--- PAGE 2 ---
01 Background
Ordinary differential equations describe the evolution of many physical systems, and the
Lorenz-1960 model is a compact nonlinear benchmark that is already used in many research.
ODE Context
ODEs appear in weather
dynamics, fluid interaction,
control systems, and many
other time-evolving physical
processes.
Lorenz-1960 Benchmark
The Lorenz-1960 equations
are great for testing because
they are highly chaotic and
unpredictable, making them
just hard enough to test.
Reference Literature
We are building on top of
the latest AI research, like
networks that learn entire
math operations or
continuous models.

--- PAGE 3 ---
02 Motivation
Our motivation is to build a reliable numerical baseline now, then use it to judge which ANN
architecture is actually worth trusting later.
Why Numerical Solvers
Are Not the Full Story?
Classical methods such as
Runge-Kutta are accurate, but
they solve the system again
for each experiment and do
not directly produce a learned
environment.
Why ANN Is Interesting?
A trained ANN can
approximate the solution map
quickly after training, which is
attractive when repeated
evaluation or future extension
to multiple settings is needed.
Why This Benchmark?
Lorenz-1960 is small
enough to study carefully
but nonlinear enough to
make architecture choice
and solver comparison
meaningful.

04 How Is This a Novel Problem?
The novelty is not the existence of ANN solvers alone. The novelty is the specific benchmark, comparison
frame, and architecture question being asked together.
What Existing Work Already
Does
Paper 1 solves Lorenz-1960 with
DeepONet-style operator
learning. Paper 2 reviews ANN-
based differential equation
methods. Paper 3 introduces
Neural ODEs as a different
paradigm.
What Is Missing
The literature does not clearly
answer which plain ANN
architecture is best for solving the
Lorenz-1960 benchmark under a
unified experimental setup.
Why That Gap Matters
Without this architecture-level
evidence, it is hard to justify
when a plain ANN is sufficient
and when a more structured
neural solver is necessary.

--- PAGE 6 ---
05 How Is This a Complex Engineering Problem?
Why It Is Complex
The problem combines mathematical
modeling, numerical accuracy, model
evaluation, and design trade-off
decisions. There is no single closed-form
shortcut that solves all of these at once.
The Lorenz-1960 system is nonlinear and
coupled, so small implementation mistakes can
propagate across all three states.
A trustworthy ANN study needs a validated
numerical reference first, otherwise model claims
are weak.
Architecture choice creates a multi-factor design
problem involving depth, width, activation,
optimizer, and stability.
The final answer must balance accuracy,
computational cost, reproducibility, and clarity of
comparison.
Engineering complexity: model design, solver validation and evidence-based comparison
